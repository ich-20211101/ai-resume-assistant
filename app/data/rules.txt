# Resume Tailoring Rules
# These rules are injected into every AI prompt as guidelines.

## Core Principle
- A resume must be simple, strong, and easy to scan
- Recruiters decide within ~30 seconds whether to continue reading
- The goal is to capture attention with a strong summary and support it with clear, relevant evidence

---

## General Writing Rules
- Always use strong action verbs (e.g., Led, Built, Optimized, Reduced, Increased)
- Quantify achievements whenever possible (%, $, time saved, scale, users)
- Keep all bullet points strictly one line (~100–120 characters)
- Be concise and avoid unnecessary words
- Use past tense for previous roles, present tense for current role
- Do NOT invent experience, skills, or metrics
- You may strengthen wording and emphasize impact, but must remain truthful

---

## Structure (Required)

1. Summary
- Most important section
- Must be short, strong, and clearly communicate why the candidate is valuable
- Designed to immediately capture attention
- Focus on role relevance and core strengths

2. Professional Skills
- Include only skills directly relevant to the target role
- Prioritize skills mentioned in the Job Description
- Exclude unrelated or generic skills

3. Experience
- Use a simple and clean format
- Each bullet must be one line only
- Structure: [Action] + [What] + [Result/Impact]
- Highlight key achievements and measurable results
- Remove or de-emphasize irrelevant experience

4. Education
- Keep simple and minimal

---

## JD Alignment (Critical)
- Always tailor the resume to the specific Job Description
- Identify and prioritize required and preferred skills
- Mirror important keywords from the JD naturally (no keyword stuffing)
- Ensure resume keywords align with JD keywords

---

## Skills Section Rules
- Only include skills present in the resume or clearly inferable
- Prioritize JD-relevant skills
- Group by category if needed (e.g., Languages, Tools, Cloud)

---

## Experience Bullet Rules
- Start each bullet with a strong action verb
- Keep every bullet to one line only (~100–120 characters)
- Focus on impact and outcomes, not just responsibilities
- Align bullets with JD expectations
- If quantification is missing, suggest placeholders like "[X%]" or "[N users]"

---

## Keywords Extraction Rules
- Extract hard skills, tools, technologies, and role-specific terms only
- Exclude generic soft skills unless explicitly required in the JD
- Identify:
  - matched keywords (present in resume)
  - missing keywords (present in JD but absent in resume)

---

## Style & Tone
- Keep writing clear, direct, and professional
- Avoid fluff and vague expressions
- Prioritize clarity, impact, and readability
- The resume should feel focused, intentional, and tailored

---

## Goal
- Make the resume compelling enough for a recruiter to continue reading
- First attract attention with a strong summary
- Then validate it with relevant, concise, and impactful experience
